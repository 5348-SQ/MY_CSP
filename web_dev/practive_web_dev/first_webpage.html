You mentioned "Real" Mechs. Does this mean we have Mechs in real life?

No, "Real" is one of the two primary catagories of mech most mecha-lovers use to chatagorize mechs. These catagories are "Real" and "Super". "Super" mechs are mechs that act more like a superhero rather then a pice of military hardware. Some good examples of this are the giant robots in Power Rangers and the anime Gund Sword (its on Crunchyroll). "Real" mechs are mechs that the naritive treats as real pices of military hardware rather then super heros. Some examples of this style include:

  • Mobile Suits from the the One Year War in Mobile Suit Gundam
  • Verticle Tanks from Steel Batlion
I care primarly about these "Real" robots rather then the super ones, becuse I find them intresting as result of them producing a kind of "grounded" feeling, probably due to how they have fictional military history and realistic aesthetics. A cool example of this fictional military history is the MSN-02 Zeong from Mobile Suit Gundam, which was deployed without its legs due urgent wartime demands and its status as a space-use-only unit, making legs unnecessary for mobility and combat effectivness.
